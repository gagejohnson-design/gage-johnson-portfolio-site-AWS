About

I bring a full-lifecycle perspective to enterprise technology—how software is evaluated, implemented, adopted, and trusted through reliable data and clear delivery. I’m a Data Management Consultant, and I’m actively building full-stack and AWS fundamentals to deepen my technical execution.

What I Do Today

In my current role, I support enterprise implementations across multiple business units. I partner with developers, architects, and business stakeholders to clarify requirements, resolve system issues, and keep delivery moving.

A big part of my work is improving data reliability—validating migrations through QA cycles, strengthening data quality, and supporting readiness so teams can operate confidently after go-live.

My Path

I started in SaaS sales, where I learned how organizations evaluate solutions and what it takes to communicate value across business and technical audiences.

From there, I moved into functional consulting and delivery—working closer to requirements, process alignment, testing cycles, and user enablement.

That progression led me to data management consulting, where I bring together business context and delivery execution to help teams launch reliable systems people actually use.

What I’m Learning and Practicing

I’m building engineering fundamentals through bootcamp projects, side work, and consistent practice. My focus is on application structure, working with APIs and databases, and improving my ability to build maintainable features end-to-end.

On the cloud side, I’m progressing through Adriaan Cantrill’s AWS Solutions Architect course with hands-on labs to strengthen how cloud environments are configured, secured, and operated.

How I Work

I work top-down—aligning on goals, user needs, and success criteria before execution. I’m comfortable bridging technical and non-technical teams, clarifying requirements early, and translating ambiguity into clear, actionable next steps.

I move iteratively, validate assumptions, and prioritize quality. I focus on the end-user experience, test functionality early, and use modern tools to troubleshoot efficiently—always aiming to deliver solutions teams can trust and adopt.

A Little More Human
